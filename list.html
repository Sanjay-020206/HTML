Book Reviews

Thinking, Fast and Slow by Kahneman
Thinking, Fast and Slow is a thought-provoking exploration of the two systems of thought that drive human decisions. Daniel Kahneman’s research unveils the cognitive biases and mental shortcuts that often lead to irrational judgments. Packed with fascinating insights and practical examples, this book helps readers understand their decision-making processes, encouraging more reflective and deliberate choices. A must-read for anyone interested in psychology, behavioral economics, and self-awareness.
Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World by Cal Newport
Deep Work is a highly practical guide to cultivating deep focus in today’s age of constant distractions. Cal Newport argues that success and productivity are increasingly dependent on our ability to concentrate without interruption. Through actionable strategies, Newport demonstrates how to eliminate distractions, manage attention, and prioritize meaningful work. A valuable resource for professionals, students, and anyone striving to achieve meaningful, high-quality output.
Educated by Tara Westover
Educated is a powerful and inspiring memoir that tells the story of Tara Westover’s journey from an isolated, abusive upbringing in rural Idaho to earning a Ph.D. from Cambridge University. Westover’s resilience, determination, and pursuit of knowledge despite overwhelming obstacles make this book a deeply moving and transformative read. It explores themes of family, self-discovery, and the pursuit of education, highlighting the power of resilience and the quest for personal freedom. A must-read for anyone seeking inspiration or reflecting on the impact of education.
